About Me

Research

Welcome! I am a full professor at the University of Hildesheim, where I am building the new Intelligent Assistive Systems (IAS) group. Our work spans both theoretical/methodological and application-driven research in deep learning, computer vision and multimodal recognition. On the methodological side, we develop new learning principles and algorithms for resource- and data-efficient deep learning, including uncertainty/OOD handling, incremental learning in open-world settings and multimodal fusion. We are also very interested in Parameter-efficient Adaptation (PEFT) of foundation models. On the applied side, we develop methods for impactful real-world problems in assistive systems, autonomous driving, robotics, and industrial (and increasingly medical) scenarios. Before joining Hildesheim, I was assistant professor at the University of Stuttgart. I am also a member of the European Laboratory for Learning and Intelligent Systems (ELLIS). Core topics include:
  • Deep Learning, Computer Vision and Multimodal Recognition
  • Multimodal and Multiscale Perception of Humans (Activities, Intentions, Emotions and Beyond)
  • Uncertainty-aware and Interpretable Recognition
  • Open Set and Open Domain Recognition, OOD Settings
  • Resource- and Data-efficient Recognition
  • Simulation- and Knowledge-enhanced Learning
  • Parameter-efficient Adaptation of Foundation Models
  • Applications for Intelligent Vehicles, Robotics, Human Assistance (e.g., Computer Vision for the Blind), Health Monitoring, Physiological Analysis and Medical Diagnostics
